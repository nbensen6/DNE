Cutting Phase Tips

Hydration

  • Drink at least 0.5-1 gallon of water daily
  • Drink a full glass before each meal to help with satiety
  • Water retention can mask fat loss - stay consistent
  • Avoid liquid calories (soda, juice) - stick to water, black coffee, tea

Sleep

  • Aim for 7-9 hours per night
  • Poor sleep increases ghrelin (hunger hormone) and cortisol
  • Keep consistent sleep/wake times
  • Avoid screens 30 min before bed

Meal Timing

  • Eat protein with every meal to preserve muscle
  • Pre-workout: light carbs + protein 1-2 hours before
  • Post-workout: protein shake within 1 hour
  • Largest meal after training if possible

Protein Priority

  • Target 150g/day (0.88g per lb bodyweight)
  • High protein preserves muscle during a caloric deficit
  • Protein is the most satiating macronutrient
  • Spread across 4-5 meals for optimal absorption
Deficit Calculation Breakdown

Your Numbers

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR): ~1,660 kcal/day (Mifflin-St Jeor for 5'6", 170 lbs male)

Activity Multiplier: 1.3x (moderate - 4x/week lifting)

Target Deficit: 400 kcal/day (moderate, sustainable)

Reading Nutrition Labels - Quick Guide

What to Check First

  • Serving Size: Everything else on the label is PER SERVING. Many packages contain 2+ servings.
  • Calories: Your primary tracking number. Aim for nutrient-dense foods under your budget.
  • Protein: Higher is better during a cut. Look for items with >20g protein per serving.
  • Fiber: Keeps you full longer. Aim for >3g per serving when possible.
  • Added Sugars: Minimize. They spike insulin and add empty calories.
  • Sodium: Causes water retention. Keep below 2,300mg/day. Watch frozen meals.
Post-Cut Bulking Preview (After June 27)

What Changes After You Hit 160 lbs

  • Calories: Increase from 1,750 to ~2,400-2,600 (lean bulk, +300-500 surplus)
  • Protein: Keep at 150g+ (stays high for muscle building)
  • Carbs: Increase significantly to fuel heavy lifts
  • Training: Shift to heavier weights, lower rep ranges (4-6), more volume
  • Cardio: Reduce HIIT to 1-2x/week, focus on lifting
  • Weight Gain Target: ~0.5 lbs/week (minimize fat gain)
  • Timeline: Bulk for 4-6 months, then re-assess
